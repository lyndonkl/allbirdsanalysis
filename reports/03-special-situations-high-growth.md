## ANALYTICAL COMMENTARY

### Why the DCF Value Is Low

The revenue-based DCF produces a modest $1.19/share for three reasons:

1. **Massive reinvestment:** Building AI compute infrastructure requires ~$700M+ in capital expenditure. Since Allbirds has only ~$80M in cash, this must come from equity issuance, which devastates per-share value through dilution.

2. **Long road to positive FCFF:** Free cash flow does not turn positive until Year 9. That means 9 years of negative cash flows being discounted at high rates (13.4% declining to 9.1%), creating a large negative PV in the explicit forecast period.

3. **All value is in the terminal value:** PV of terminal value ($1,079M) is the only source of positive value, offset by ($449M) in explicit-period negative FCFFs. This makes the valuation extremely sensitive to terminal assumptions.

### Why the EV/Sales Relative Valuation Is Higher

The relative valuation produces higher numbers ($3.57-$8.86) because:

1. It assumes the market will value Allbirds as an AI compute company by Year 5, applying high-growth multiples to a revenue base that is growing rapidly.
2. It implicitly assumes the company successfully raises capital and executes the pivot -- survivorship bias is embedded in the comparable universe (CoreWeave, Lambda, etc. are survivors).
3. Market multiples for AI compute are extraordinarily elevated as of 2025, reflecting euphoria that may not persist.

### The Fundamental Challenge

Even under this thesis-optimistic framework with favorable assumptions at every turn:
- Low failure probability (20% vs. a more realistic 60-80%)
- Favorable capital raise terms (250M new shares vs. potentially 400M+)
- High target margins (25%, top quartile)
- Rapid revenue ramp ($1.2B by Year 10)

The DCF intrinsic value is only $1.19/share. This tells us something important: **radical pivots by small, cash-strapped companies are extremely difficult to create shareholder value even when they succeed**, because the dilution from capital raises transfers most of the value created to new investors rather than existing shareholders.

The optimistic case for existing shareholders requires either:
- Raising capital at high valuations (requires market excitement about the pivot)
- Finding a strategic partner who provides capital without equity dilution
- Successfully bootstrapping with minimal capital (unlikely for GPU infrastructure)
